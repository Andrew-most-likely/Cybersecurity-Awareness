## Phishing

Phishing is a type of attack where an adversary impersonates a trusted entity to steal credentials or deliver malware.

**How to spot phishing:**
- Unexpected urgency ("Your account will be closed!")
- Mismatched sender domains (e.g. `support@paypa1.com`)
- Links that hover to a different URL than displayed
- Requests for passwords, SSNs, or payment info via email

**What to do:**
- Never click links in unexpected emails — go directly to the site
- Report phishing emails to your IT/security team
- Use email filters and anti-phishing browser extensions

## Password Security

Weak or reused passwords are one of the most common causes of account compromise.

**Best practices:**
- Use a password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password, KeePass)
- Minimum 16 characters with mixed case, numbers, and symbols
- Never reuse passwords across sites
- Change passwords immediately if a breach is suspected

## Social Engineering

Social engineering exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.

**Common tactics:**
- **Pretexting** — attacker invents a scenario to extract info
- **Baiting** — leaving infected USB drives in public places
- **Vishing** — voice phishing over the phone
- **Tailgating** — physically following authorized personnel into secure areas

**Defense:**
- Verify identities before sharing sensitive information
- Follow your organization's information handling policies
- When in doubt, escalate to your security team

## Safe Browsing

**Tips:**
- Only visit HTTPS sites (look for the padlock icon)
- Avoid downloading software from unofficial sources
- Keep your browser and extensions up to date
- Use a reputable ad blocker (uBlock Origin)
- Be cautious on public Wi-Fi — use a VPN

---

## Two-Factor Authentication

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) adds a second layer of verification beyond your password.

**Recommended methods (most to least secure):**
1. Hardware security key (YubiKey)
2. Authenticator app (Aegis, Google Authenticator, Authy)
3. SMS code (better than nothing, but vulnerable to SIM swapping)

**Enable 2FA on:**
- Email accounts
- Banking and financial services
- Social media
- Any account with sensitive personal data
